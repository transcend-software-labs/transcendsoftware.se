# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

CTOs and Heads of Engineering at funded startups and scale-ups. They are technically literate decision-makers evaluating contractors for senior engineering or architecture work. They visit the site while considering whether to reach out, often with a specific problem in mind (scaling issues, CI/CD chaos, architecture debt). They have little patience for vague claims or marketing copy.

## Product Purpose

A personal landing page and blog for Rasmus Kockum, a senior software engineer and tech lead offering consulting engagements. The site must convince a technically savvy visitor to send an email within a single session. The blog builds SEO and demonstrates expertise over time. Success means the visitor reads the hero, scans the services, and reaches for their keyboard.

## Brand Personality

Precise, Expert, Direct. No self-promotion theater. Confidence through specificity, not adjectives. The site should read like it was written by someone who has solved the problem the visitor is staring at.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing page: no feature grids, hero-metric templates, or CTA overload
- Corporate consultant site: no stock photos, suit-and-tie tone, buzzword soup, or vague "transformation" language
- Over-engineered dev portfolio: no terminal animations, cursor effects, excessive interactivity, or showoff JS

## Design Principles

1. **Specificity over superlatives.** Every claim earns its place with a number or a concrete detail ("20k RPS", "since 2016"). Remove anything a competitor could copy-paste onto their site unchanged.
2. **The site practices what it preaches.** A consultant selling engineering quality must have an engineering-quality site: fast, clean, no dead weight.
3. **Friction to conversion must be zero.** The email address is never more than a scroll away. No forms, no calendars, no qualification steps.
4. **Let the work speak.** Real project history and real blog posts outperform any amount of brand copy.
5. **Earned confidence, not performed confidence.** Tone is calm and direct. Never hype. Never hedging.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline good practice: readable contrast, keyboard navigable, semantic HTML. No specific WCAG level mandated.
